OBJECTIVE


To deliver innovative, sustainable, and functional architectural solutions that enhance the built environment and improve the quality of life for every need. Through a commitment to design excellence, creative problem-solving, and client collaboration, we strive to create spaces that reflect the unique needs, values, and visions of our clients.


PHILOSOPHY


THEATRICAL EXPERIENCES


We use the natural sunlight to render the spaces within. Such spaces satisfy psychological and physical function of a space and offer memories. Spaces under the influence of natural sunlight give a very theatrical experience to the user and eventually become one with the space.

The play of levels in our spaces further enhances this experience and one is completely disconnected from the outside world and begins to belong to a world of its own.

ELEGANCE IN SIMPLICITY


A complexity of emotions is formed without having to add a lot of elements together, as we improve the relationship between the elements in immediate proximity. These areas encourage user participation, which leads to the establishment of a relationship with the user. Engagement and elegance in the built form are what come from here.

LONGER VISTAS


By creating longer vistas where the eye can travel farther than expected and satisfy the nature of the eye, we hope to create an image of a space that is in conflict with the actual canvas of land. Consequently, the user is given the impression that the space is larger than anticipated. Enhancing the user's experience and aspirations while the spaces are being used is crucial.

STORIES IN ARCHITECTURE


A story or stories are formed at different levels of space and time continuum as a result of the complexity of emotions and relationships among architecture. These stories can be interpreted in a variety of ways depending on the references they are examined and experienced at.

In architecture, a story must be told; nevertheless, the story may evolve throughout time.

WITH THE MASTER


Working on the Master's numerous projects in Punjab, particularly in Chandigarh, with Ar. Aman Sohal, one of the most renowned practitioners of modern Indian architecture in Chandigarh, was an immense blessing. For his project JDCA (Jatin Das Centre for Arts, Bhubaneswar), Aman Sir also collaborated with B. V. Doshi, the Father of Indian architecture. Doshi was selected as the 2018 Laureate by the Pritzker Prize committee nearly nine decades later. B.V. Doshi was an architectural apprentice to Le Corbusier in his Paris atelier from 1951 to 1955. The master plan for JDCA was conceived by Aman Sohal sir and Doshi sir on the natural outcrops of a land across from the Udaigiri and Khandagiri caves. The land had a natural terrain and the plan was to configure requirements like exhibition spaces, collections of various crafts, Pankhas , sculptures and other rare artifacts by a renowned Artist Jatin Das.
